PSZAER · Institutional opinions

What the official bodies said —
and how the Government replied

Before the public consultation even opened, Portugal's own public bodies were formally consulted on the PSZAER. Several gave unfavourable or heavily conditional opinions — and the Government's own weighing document shows nearly two thirds of the contributions were only partly accepted or merely noted, with no change to the programme.

Source, translated and summarised in plain English from: “Análise e Ponderação dos Pareceres das Entidades” (EMER 2030, 23 June 2026, 47 pp.). This is our faithful summary, not an official translation.

What it means

The people who guard our heritage, farmland and nature already objected

The heritage authority, the agriculture authority, the national association of municipalities and the nature-conservation institute (ICNF) all pushed back — ICNF explicitly asking for the zones around the Serra da Gardunha protected landscape to be removed. Yet the Government's weighing accepted most concerns only “in part” or simply “noted” them. The public consultation is the next chance to be heard — and citizens' objections must be weighed too.
